Treatment Performed

Initial cleanout treatment was performed throughout the entire unit. Treatment addressed all confirmed harborage areas including appliance voids and motors, cabinetry, pantry tracks, cracks and crevices, wall voids, door frames, baseboards, and all room areas where live activity was documented. Roaches were flushed from nesting sites during the course of treatment. A significant number of roaches were removed as a result of the initial service.

Recommendations & Follow-Up Plan
⚠ Follow-Up Treatment Required
Due to the severity and widespread nature of this infestation, a minimum of 1 to 2 additional treatments will be required to achieve full eradication. German Roach infestations of this level — involving all life stages and multiple established nesting colonies — cannot be resolved with a single treatment. Eggs already deposited within the unit will continue to hatch following the initial service.

Recommended follow-up window: 3–5 days from initial service (by April 11–13, 2026). Timely follow-up is critical to intercept emerging nymphs before they mature and re-establish harborage.
⚑ Advisory — Pre-Tenant Turnover Cleanup
A notable quantity of dead roaches were removed as a result of treatment. However, it is anticipated that additional dead roaches will continue to appear in the days following service as the treatment takes full effect. Pest Pro strongly recommends that the property be thoroughly cleaned — including behind and beneath all appliances, inside all cabinets, drawers, and closets — prior to turning the unit over to an incoming tenant. This recommendation is advisory in nature; Pest Pro's scope of work is limited to pest control services only.
⚑ Advisory — Cabinetry Gaps & Sanitation
Unsealed gaps were identified in cabinetry below the sink, providing direct access to wall voids. Pest Pro recommends that these gaps be sealed prior to or concurrent with follow-up treatment to reduce reharborage potential and improve treatment efficacy. Additionally, the sanitation conditions observed behind the refrigerator (grease buildup, food debris) contributed significantly to harborage conditions and should be addressed during the cleaning process.
